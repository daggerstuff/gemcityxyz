This blog has been a long time coming. Not because I didn't have things to say — trust me, I have _opinions_ — but because I needed to figure out exactly what I wanted this space to be.

## What This Is

**Gem City** is my digital embassy. A place where:

- AI and LLM discourse happens without the hype cycle bullshit
- Tech culture gets the critical examination it desperately needs
- Activist technology is taken seriously as engineering practice
- Code and creativity coexist without corporate sanitization

<Callout variant="tip">
  If you're looking for "10x developer tips" or "how to optimize your morning
  routine for maximum hustle," you're in the wrong place. Try Medium.
</Callout>

## What You'll Find Here

**Sharp AI Analysis** — What LLMs actually do (spoiler: not intelligence), how to build with them effectively, and why most "AI startups" are going to fail spectacularly.

**Tech Culture Critique** — The surveillance state runs on APIs. The 10x developer myth is killing innovation. Open source as resistance. These aren't just thinkpieces — they're battle reports from the front lines.

**Activist Engineering** — Practical guides for building technology that serves liberation rather than extraction. From [acab.lol](https://acab.lol) to security for organizers, this is tech with teeth.

**The Creative Thread** — The music, art, and unfiltered expression that once lived at vivi.rocks now calls gemcity.xyz home. Code as craft. Systems as art. The rhythm in the machine.

## The Digital Empire

This blog is just one node in a larger constellation:

- **gemcity.xyz** — This nexus. You're here. The home for tech discourse, creative expression, and everything in between.
- **pixelatedempathy.com** — Enterprise AI for emotional intelligence. My flagship project.
- **chadisfaction.tech** — The technical playground.
- **acab.lol** — Activist tech and security research.

- **bantesla.lol** — Critical tech analysis with a satirical edge.

Each serves a purpose. Together, they form the landscape I operate within.

## The Voice

I write like I talk: sharp, occasionally profane, allergic to corporate buzzwords. I believe:

- Technology should empower the marginalized, not entrench the powerful
- Every line of code is a political act
- AI is fascinating and dangerous in equal measure
- The best systems are built by people who question the systems they're building

If that resonates, stick around. If it doesn't, no hard feelings — the internet is vast and there's a Substack for everyone.
